Beating the Stereotype

by Christina Quach

“It almost became a norm for me, as if animated frames and a serious story couldn’t exist at the same time.”
INTRO

If you’ve ever mentioned watching anime out loud, you’ve probably heard someone laugh and say, “Ew!! anime’s for kids only!” When you started watching during the pandemic, that phrase showed up everywhere online.

HISTORY

Anime wasn’t taken seriously as a subject of Western academics until the early 2000s, when researchers started examining it across multiple fields. Over time, scholarship split into different approaches, from literary interpretations to media theory.

IMPACT

The stereotype discourages people from giving anime a fair chance. When something can build communities and inspire reflection, calling it childish starts sounding less like critique and more like refusal to understand.
